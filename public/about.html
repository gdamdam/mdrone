What it is

An instrument built around one sustained tone. Layered voices, slow weather, microtonal tuning, a long reverb tail, and two slow modulators, so a held note stays interesting for hours. No clock, no beats, no quantize.

What is a drone?

A drone is one sustained tone, or a small set of tones, held for a long time. There's no melody, no beat. A note simply stays, and its colour shifts slowly underneath. You've heard drones all your life: the hum under Indian classical music, the bagpipe's low note, the ringing of Tibetan bowls, the long chords of ambient records.

What does microtonal mean?

A piano splits the octave into twelve equal steps. Most of the world's music doesn't. mdrone ships twenty-two tuning systems: Pythagorean, Kirnberger III, just intonation, 31-TET, Yaman, Bayati, and more. A sustained fifth sounds one way in one tuning, then a noticeably different way when you switch.

Why in a browser?

No install, no account, no sign-up. The sound is synthesised on your device. Nothing is uploaded. Sessions live in your browser. Scenes share as URLs, not as server-hosted files. Works on any modern phone, tablet, or laptop, and it's free.
